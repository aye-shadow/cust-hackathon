Margalla Hills National Park hosts several notable mammal species. Here are some of the key mammals found in the area:

1. Common Asian Leopard (Panthera pardus)
- Occasionally spotted in remote areas
- Most active during dawn and dusk
- Recent sightings reported near Trail 6
- Primarily feeds on barking deer and wild boar

2. Barking Deer/Indian Muntjac (Muntiacus muntjak)
- Common in forested areas
- Known for their distinctive bark-like call
- Most active during early morning and late evening
- Often seen near water sources

3. Wild Boar (Sus scrofa)
- Abundant throughout the park
- More active at night
- Often seen in groups
- Important prey species for leopards

4. Rhesus Macaque (Macaca mulatta)
- Very common throughout the hills
- Live in large troops
- Often encountered near hiking trails
- Can be aggressive if fed by humans

5. Indian Palm Civet (Paradoxurus hermaphroditus)
- Nocturnal species
- Rarely seen during day
- Important seed disperser
- Found in forested areas

Conservation Status:
- The leopard population is considered vulnerable
- Habitat fragmentation is a major concern
- Human-wildlife conflict needs careful management
- Several conservation programs are active in the area
